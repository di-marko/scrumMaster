What is Agile? What is Scrum?

Agile is a mindset: a set of values and principles. These values and principles are presented in the Agile Manifesto .

Scrum is a way in which this mindset can be applied. It is an iterative approach where a team of 4-6 people regularly deliver a working product (feature) that meets the user's stories.

You can say that Scrum is Agile, however, Agile doesn't have to be Scrum.

Roles

Product Owner

This person closely works with the stakeholders and customers. He or she decides WHAT needs to be worked on and WHY it needs to be worked on.

Scrum Master

This person is responsible for the whole Scrum process to be run smoothly and incrementally.

Development Team

Based on the Product Owner's priority list the development team developes the product. The work is planned within the team and all progress is tracked for future improvements.

Artifacts & Backlog

According to the Atlassian: "Agile scrum artifacts are information that a scrum team and stakeholders use to detail the product being developed, actions to produce it, and the actions performed during the project. The main agile scrum artifacts are product backlog, sprint backlog, and increments."

Product Backlog, in other words User Stories, is managed by the Product Owner and describes wishes (requirements) that the stakeholder wants to have for the product.

Sprint Backlog is managed by Development Team and it is created during Sprint Planning.

Sprint

It is like a living organism with it's own breathing rhythm. This rhythm is usually between 2 to 4 weeks, however, it can vary (1-2, 2-3 weeks) depending on the team and organizational preferences.

During a Sprint the Scrum Team plans, develops, reviews and iterates the product development.

Sprint Planning

Sprint Planning A Product Owner presents main idea of what has to be done and the Development Team creates a plan from the next Sprint.

Sprint Review

During this stage the Product Owner with the Scrum Team present the sprint results to the customers or stakeholders.

Sprint Retrospective

This stage is done right after the Sprint Review where the Scrum Master sees over what can be improved for the next Sprint, such as team collaboration.

Agile Estimation

As the name leads, this is where the Scrum Team estimates time necessary to complete tasks in the product backlog.
